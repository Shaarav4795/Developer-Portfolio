AI tools are strongest at generating options, not choosing the right one. I treat them like rapid collaborators: useful for exploring many paths, but never responsible for final decisions.

For UI work, I ask for three distinct visual directions with explicit constraints. Then I evaluate them against brand clarity, accessibility, and implementation cost. This turns AI output into a decision framework instead of random inspiration.

For writing, I use AI to challenge assumptions and surface blind spots. I keep voice and structure manual. That keeps the final piece aligned with my intent while still benefiting from breadth.

The principle is simple: speed from AI, standards from humans.
